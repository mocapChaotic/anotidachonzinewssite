Breakthrough in Cancer Research at City Hospital

Researchers at City Medical Center have announced a promising new approach to treating aggressive forms of cancer. The team, led by Dr. Lisa Reynolds, has developed a targeted therapy that shows significant tumor reduction in early trials.

"This represents a potential paradigm shift in how we approach cancer treatment," Dr. Reynolds explained. "Our method targets cancer cells with unprecedented precision while sparing healthy tissue."

The research, which has been underway for seven years, received $12 million in funding from the National Institutes of Health and private donors. The next phase of clinical trials will begin later this year.

Dr. Lisa Reynolds

Lead Researcher, City Medical Center
